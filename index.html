Miranda Heath

Hello! I'm Miranda, a psychologist and philosopher studying for a PhD at the University of Edinburgh.

I am currently exploring how individualism in moral psychology limits our understanding of what it means to be good to one another in ways that reproduce and reinforce market liberal ideology. I hope that recognising this will help us gain a richer understanding of ther ways in which we can stand in caring relation to one another—one that enables us to live well together and better respond to existential collective action problems like the climate crisis.

I am interested in how ideology shapes the sense we (can) make of the world. I co-organise the Ideology Research Group at the University of Edinburgh, where we present and discuss both new work and existing literature on ideology broadly construed.

Want to know a bit more about me? Check out my about page and my socials!

Methodological Approach

I use qualitative, quantitative and philosophical methods. I am particularly interested in the critical analysis of discourse and post-positivist and critical realist metholdologies that help us identify and subvert existing power structures.

Lab

I am part of the Altruistic Minds Lab, run by my supervisor, Dr. Matti Wilks. We meet bi-weekly to discuss topics in moral psychology, from the moral circle to the moral treatment of animals to morality and ethics in AI. We welcome external speakers where possible!

Knowledge, Skills and Experience

I am experienced in qualitative data collection (interviews, focus groups, open questionnaires) and analysis (particularly critical discourse studies, post-structuralist discourse theory, discursive psyschology and thematic analysis).

I have conducted research in social and moral psychology, applied philosophy, philosophy of mind and health and education research. Professionally, I have explored reflection and threshold concepts in medical and dental education, and how prospective donors and renal staff discuss non-directed kidney donation in the UK.

I am trained to a post-graduate level in qualitative methods, univariate and multivariate statistics in R, Bayesian statistics in R and research skills and design.
